## User personas – Country Analytics Platform

### Persona 1 – Regional Strategy Lead (primary)

- **Role**: VP / Director of Strategy for a regional business unit (e.g. APAC, EMEA).
- **Goals**:
  - Quickly understand which countries are growing fastest in GDP and population.
  - Prepare slides and narratives for quarterly business and board reviews.
  - Identify “outlier” markets that merit deeper attention or investment.
- **Pain points**:
  - Raw datasets are time‑consuming to clean.
  - BI dashboards in the organisation are often slow or narrowly scoped.
  - Needs something fast and reliable for “day‑before‑the‑meeting” questions.
- **Success criteria**:
  - Can answer “Where are our top 10 growth markets?” in under 5 minutes.
  - Can screenshot or export clear visuals that executives understand immediately.

### Persona 2 – Country Economist / Policy Analyst

- **Role**: Economist in a central bank, ministry, multilateral organisation, or think tank.
- **Goals**:
  - Track medium‑to‑long‑term trends in GDP, demographics, and life expectancy.
  - Compare one country to its peers and to the global distribution.
  - Spot structural shifts (ageing population, slowing GDP per capita, etc.).
- **Pain points**:
  - Existing tools are either too simplistic (static charts) or too heavy (full statistical packages).
  - Wants strong defaults around data quality and clear methodology, not ad‑hoc calculations.
- **Success criteria**:
  - Can quickly validate whether a hypothesis is consistent with WDI data.
  - Can export or re‑create key visuals in research reports with minimal manual work.

### Persona 3 – Market Expansion Manager

- **Role**: Corporate manager responsible for geographic expansion.
- **Goals**:
  - Compare market size and growth across potential target countries.
  - Look at population age structure to infer demand for different product lines.
  - Share simple, data‑driven stories with non‑technical executives.
- **Pain points**:
  - Raw economics data is hard to connect with product / marketing decisions.
  - Needs a narrative‑friendly, non‑technical interface.
- **Success criteria**:
  - Can shortlist 3–5 countries based on GDP, GDP per capita, and demographics.
  - Can answer follow‑ups during meetings without needing a data team.

### Persona 4 – Data / BI Analyst

- **Role**: Analyst or data engineer supporting strategy, finance, or policy teams.
- **Goals**:
  - Use the app as a **first‑pass exploration tool** before building deeper models.
  - Validate that new data integrations or local datasets are consistent with WDI ranges.
- **Pain points**:
  - Prior work often involves repetitive Jupyter / BI setup for simple exploratory questions.
  - Maintains many temporary, one‑off visualisations.
- **Success criteria**:
  - Fewer bespoke exploratory notebooks and ad‑hoc visualisations.
  - Can provide stakeholders with a link to the app instead of screenshots of spreadsheets.
